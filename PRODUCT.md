# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Self-hosted job seekers — people in the thick of an active job hunt who've chosen to run their own instance rather than use a commercial tracker. They are organised enough to self-host, which means they're deliberate and process-oriented. They check this daily, often multiple times a day. The stakes are real: a missed follow-up or a forgotten application has consequences. They need the tool to feel like it's *on their side* — not just recording data, but building momentum.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: tactical, forward, sharp**

This is a campaign tool, not a CRM. Job hunting is a campaign you run against a market — it requires tracking progress, recognising patterns, and maintaining forward motion through rejection. The interface should feel like a well-organised campaign headquarters: purposeful, no wasted space, information that rewards attention.

Emotional goal: the user should feel like they're *making progress*, even on days when nothing moves. The visual system should celebrate pipeline advancement and make the complexity of a job search feel manageable.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Bold, editorial, typographically driven.** Think mission board meets editorial spread — not a corporate SaaS tool and not a pastel personal journal.

- Strong typographic hierarchy where company names and role titles feel like they *matter*
- A limited, intentional palette — not "safety grey" but a deliberate neutral tinted toward the accent
- High contrast, not low contrast. Legibility is a feature.
- System-adaptive: light and dark modes, both fully designed (not one as an afterthought)
- Motion: purposeful — stage transitions should feel like *advancement*, not just animation
- Anti-references: Notion (too editorial/document-like), Linear (too cold), Trello (too toy-like), generic admin dashboards

**What makes it unforgettable**: The pipeline stages feel alive — advancing an application from "Applied" to "Interview" should feel like a small victory.

### Design Principles
1. **Forward motion is the hero** — every design decision should reinforce the sense of a campaign advancing, not stalling.
2. **Information density earns its keep** — show what matters, hide what doesn't, never show both equally.
3. **Colour carries meaning** — stage colours aren't decoration, they're a system. Green means progress, red means closed, the rest are states in between.
4. **Both themes are first-class** — no light mode with dark mode as a half-hearted inversion. Design for both contexts.
5. **No AI aesthetics** — no gradient text, no side-stripe cards, no glassmorphism, no hero metrics. Distinctive over safe.
